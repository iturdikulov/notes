Hello, world! This is CS50 (aka CS50x through edX), Harvard University's introduction to the intellectual enterprises of computer science and the art of programming.

You are welcome to take this course at your own pace, starting whenever you'd like. So no worries if this is your first time here!

If you started but did not finish CS50x in 2022, not to worry! Your work will carry over, as per the FAQs!

Much awaits you, so here's how to begin!

First join some of CS50's communities, so that you can reach out to classmates and staff with questions:

  1. If you're the YouTube type:

  2. If you're the TikTok type:
    • follow @cs50
    • follow @davidjmalan

After that, watch each of the course's lectures and submit the course's labs, problem sets, and final project:

And if you have any questions, start a discussion with classmates!

Looking for other courses to take before or after this one?
  • CS50x: Introduction to Computer Science, an introduction to the intellectual enterprises of computer science and the art of programming.
  • CS50AI: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence with Python, an introductory course in artificial intelligence in which you'll learn to use machine learning in Python. Assumes that you've taken CS50x.
  • CS50B: Computer Science for Business Professionals, CS50's introduction to computer science for business professionals.
  • CS50G: Introduction to Game Development, in which you'll learn about the development of 2D and 3D interactive games as you explore the design of games such as Super Mario Bros., Pokmon, Angry Birds, and more. Assumes that you've taken CS50x.
  • CS50L: CS50 for Lawyers, a variant of Harvard University's introduction to computer science, CS50, designed especially for lawyers (and law students).
  • CS50P: Introduction to Programming with Python, an introduction to programming using a language called Python, which you can take before CS50x, during CS50x, or after CS50x.
  • CS50S: Introduction to Programming with Scratch, a gentle introduction to programming that prepares you for subsequent courses in coding.
  • CS50T: Understanding Technology, CS50's introduction to technology for students who don't (yet!) consider themselves computer persons.
  • CS50W: Web Programming with Python and JavaScript, in which you'll dive more deeply into the design and implementation of web apps with Python, JavaScript, and SQL using frameworks like Django, React, and Bootstrap. Assumes that you've taken CS50x.
